I · The problem

A diploma is only as good as the trust behind it.

Credentials can be forged. Verifying one means calling a registrar, trusting a database, hoping the record still exists. The proof lives somewhere else — not with the document.

What if a credential carried its own proof — checkable by anyone, anywhere, with no server to ask?

II · The trust spine — aion-context

Every record is signed, chained, and verifiable offline.

aion-edu is built on aion-context: a tamper-evident, Ed25519-signed, hash-chained ledger. Rubrics, credentials, accreditations — each is a cryptographic record whose history cannot be altered without breaking the chain.

Tamper with one byte and the signature fails. Trust travels with the document.

V · The institutional layer

A new layer for the universities you already trust.

aion-edu doesn't replace the institution — it gives it a new layer. A university brings its curriculum and its rubrics; aion-edu supplies AI faculty that teach them live, at any scale; and aion-context seals every credential to the school's own standard — one shared, verifiable record of truth that students, employers, and other institutions can all check.

1 · Expose

Your curriculum

Publish your courses, outcomes, and grading rubrics. Each becomes a signed record on aion-context — the canonical standard, owned by you.

2 · Teach

AI faculty, at scale

Master-teacher AI delivers your curriculum live — reasoning with each student and grading against your exact rubric, for one learner or a hundred thousand.

3 · Seal

Shared accuracy

Every credential is bound to the rubric it was earned against. No drift, no dispute — one source of truth, verifiable offline by anyone.
